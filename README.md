# Make_Stationary

MATLAB implementation of **"Does Stationarization Help? A Frugal Benchmark of Deseasonalization for Renewable Energy Forecasting"**.

Two frugal, phase-conditioned Extreme Learning Machine (ELM) operators - **PROJECTION** and **PHASE** - are used as controlled instruments to test whether removing the deterministic diurnal/annual cycle of a renewable-energy or meteorological time series actually improves downstream forecasting, rather than assuming it does. Both operators estimate the seasonal component in closed form from a single annual cycle, with no harmonic specification and no iterative tuning. They are benchmarked against a crossed **FOURIER** regression, a **LOESS** smoother, and a non-parametric **rolling-median** template, and against direct forecasting on the **RAW** signal, over nine variables (GHI, T2M, wind speed, PV, wind power, total electrical load) and forecast horizons from 1 to 6 h.

**Main finding**: the two proposed operators and the rolling median produce the most stationary residuals, but a cleaner residual is not, by itself, a more predictable one. Deseasonalization helps mainly for strongly cyclostationary variables at longer horizons (up to 4 pp nRMSE reduction for 30-min GHI, 3 pp for hourly wind speed), is neutral for already-regular short-horizon signals, and is mildly counterproductive for PV, total load, and wind power. **PHASE**, the lighter of the two operators, is the only one that never degrades forecasting.

### Pipeline order

The three folders are meant to run **in sequence**, each consuming the previous step's output:

1. **`Optimization/optimization_all_variables.m`** grid-searches `(L, m)` per variable and writes `results_optimization_all_variables/Best_Optimization_AllVariables.csv`.
2. Copy/link that CSV next to **`Deseasonalization/main_deseasonalization.m`** as `Best_Optimization_AllVariables.csv` (already included in the repo, pre-computed). Running it deseasonalizes all 9 variables with all 6 methods and writes one `results_final_pipeline/<Variable>_FULL.csv` per variable (raw signal + seasonal component + residual, per method) plus `results_final_pipeline/Metrics.csv` (PACF/entropy/LLE per method and variable).
3. **`Forecasting/main_forecast_GHI_and_GHI_30min.m`** and **`Forecasting/main_forecasting_PV_WT_PT_WS_T2M.m`** read those `*_FULL.csv` files, forecast the residual of each method with both an ELM and an AR model, add the seasonal component back, and write per-variable, per-horizon metrics and forecasts.

## Methodology

### 1. Cyclostationary decomposition

The observed signal is decomposed additively, $Y_t = S_t + R_t$, where $S_t$ is the repeatable phase-dependent (seasonal) component and $R_t$ the residual. The additive form is used instead of a multiplicative one because $S_t$ is exactly zero at night for GHI and PV, which would make a ratio-based (clear-sky-index-style) decomposition singular.

### 2. Phase encoding

Two normalized phase variables encode the diurnal and annual cycles:

$$\varphi_t = \frac{t \bmod T_d}{T_d}, \qquad \psi_t = \frac{t \bmod T_y}{T_y}$$

where $T_d$ and $T_y$ are the daily and annual periods expressed in samples (e.g. $T_d=24$, $T_y=365\times24$ for hourly data; the code also handles a 15-min/96-sample-per-day case). Each phase is embedded through a sine-cosine pair to avoid discontinuities at cycle boundaries:

$$p_t = \big[\cos(2\pi\varphi_t),\ \sin(2\pi\varphi_t),\ \cos(2\pi\psi_t),\ \sin(2\pi\psi_t)\big]$$

### 3. ELM architecture and ridge regression

The signal is standardized on training statistics only, $Z_t = (Y_t-\mu_{tr})/\sigma_{tr}$. For the **PROJECTION** operator, the input combines $L{+}1$ lags of $Z_t$ with the phase embedding, $x_t=[Z_t,\dots,Z_{t-L},p_t]$; for **PHASE**, the input is the phase embedding alone, $x_t=p_t$. A single random hidden layer is drawn once and kept fixed:

$$H=\tanh(XW+b), \qquad W\sim\mathcal U(-1,1),\ b\sim\mathcal U(-1,1)$$

Output weights are obtained by ridge regression, in closed form:

$$\beta=\arg\min_\beta \lVert H\beta-z\rVert_2^2+\lambda\lVert\beta\rVert_2^2 \iff (H^\top H+\lambda I)\beta=H^\top z$$

solved via Cholesky factorization (or an SVD fallback when the system is rank-deficient — see `ols_fit.m`).

### 4. Phase projection at inference

Once trained, **PROJECTION** estimates the seasonal component by neutralizing all lagged inputs to their standardized training mean (zero) while keeping the phase embedding: $\tilde x_t=[0,\dots,0,p_t]$, so $\hat S_t=f(\tilde x_t)\cdot\sigma_{tr}+\mu_{tr}$. **PHASE** needs no such neutralization since it never sees lagged inputs. For non-negative, solar-like variables (GHI, PV), the seasonal estimate is clipped, $\hat S_t\to\max(0,\hat S_t)$; this is not applied to temperature, wind, or load.

### 5. Hyperparameter selection

The paper's Level-I/Level-II hierarchical scheme selects, in order, the ridge parameter $\lambda\in\{10^{-6},\dots,10^{3}\}$ and then the architecture $(L,m)$ by minimizing the aggregated residual PACF,

$$\mathrm{PACF}(\hat R)=\sum_{k=1}^{K}\big|\mathrm{PACF}_{\hat R}(k)\big|,\qquad K=48\text{ samples}$$

**Implementation note (paper vs. code):** in both `Make_stationary_all_variables.m` files the ridge parameter is not actually grid-searched — it is hardcoded to $\lambda=10^{-4}$ — and only $(L,m)$ are searched, over `LagH_base = 12:12:60` (hourly, doubled to `24:24:120` for the 30-min sets) and `m_list = 100:100:1600` (`Optimization/optimization_all_variables.m`). This grid search is what produces `Best_Optimization_AllVariables.csv` (Table below). This README describes what the code does, which departs slightly from the paper's Level-I ridge search.

Optimal configurations retained by the grid search (Table I of the paper):

| Variable    | Optimal lag $L$ (h) | Optimal neurons $m$ |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| PV          | 24                  | 600                  |
| WT          | 48                  | 600                  |
| PT          | 24                  | 1600                 |
| GHI_30min   | 48                  | 1200                 |
| T2M_30min   | 144                 | 800                  |
| GHI_1h      | 48                  | 600                  |
| T2M_1h      | 24                  | 800                  |
| WS_30min    | 48                  | 1200                 |
| WS_1h       | 24                  | 600                  |

**A second implementation note:** `Optimization/Make_stationary_all_variables.m` and `Deseasonalization/Make_stationary_all_variables.m` share the same name but are **not the same function**:

- `Optimization/Make_stationary_all_variables.m` — used only by the grid search. $K=500$ random hidden-layer draws per configuration, retained by **Spearman rank correlation** between the reconstructed seasonal component and the observed signal; solar-like variables get an additional grid-searched rescaling factor $\alpha\in[0.01,3]$ applied to the seasonal estimate.
- `Deseasonalization/Make_stationary_all_variables.m` — used by the final pipeline (`main_deseasonalization.m`) that produces the results reported in the paper. $K=300$ draws, retained by minimizing the **lag-24 autocorrelation of the residual** (a lighter proxy for the PACF criterion), and followed by an **affine recalibration** $S_{\text{cal}}=a\cdot S+b$ fitted by OLS on the training set (not described in the paper's equations) before the residual is formed.

Because both files share the same name, **do not add both `Optimization/` and `Deseasonalization/` to the MATLAB path at the same time** — whichever comes first on the path silently shadows the other. Run each stage from its own folder.

### 6. Benchmark deseasonalization methods

- **FOURIER** — crossed diurnal x annual harmonic regression with ridge regularization:

$$S^{\text{FOURIER}}_t=\sum_{k=1}^{K_F}\sum_{\ell=0}^{L_F}\Big[a_k\cos(2\pi k\varphi_t)+b_k\sin(2\pi k\varphi_t)\Big]\,c_\ell(\psi_t)$$

  (`crossed_fourier_deseason.m`, $K=L=3$ harmonics, $\lambda=10^{-2}$, fitted on the training year only).

- **LOESS** — `smoothdata(x, 'loess', window)` fitted on the training year, MATLAB built-in.

- **Rolling median** — for each hour-of-day $h$, an empirical template over a $\pm10$-day circular window:

$$T_h(d)=\mathrm{median}\big(\{G_h(d-10),\dots,G_h(d+10)\}\big)$$

  (`rolling_median_deseason.m`).

### 7. Forecasting wrapper

Each method's residual $\hat R_t$ (and the RAW signal itself, as a sixth "no deseasonalization" case) is forecast independently by two models, then the seasonal component is added back to reconstruct the forecast in physical units:

$$\hat Y_{t+h}=\hat S_{t+h}+\hat R_{t+h}$$

- **ELM forecaster** (`Train_EL.m` / `Pred_EL_R.m`): a **mixed-activation** ELM — a fraction $T=0.6$ of hidden neurons use a sigmoid activation, the rest a Gaussian $\exp(-a^2/2)$ — trained by ridge regression ($\lambda=0.2$) over 96 random hidden-layer draws, keeping the lowest-training-RMSE draw. This architecture is a code-level implementation detail not spelled out in the paper's equations (which describe the *deseasonalization* ELM, not the *forecasting* one).
- **AR forecaster** (`Train_AR.m` / `Pred_AR_R.m`): plain linear OLS on the same lagged residual inputs, no regularization.
- Inputs are `Nin` lags of the (standardized) residual; `Nin`/`Nh` (hidden neurons) are fixed per horizon (`InputOutput_R.m`, hardcoded per-horizon table in both `main_forecast*.m` scripts):

  | Horizon (h) | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 (or other) |
  |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
  | `Nin` | 52 | 50 | 49 | 51 | 46 | 72 |
  | `Nh`  | 220 | 230 | 220 | 170 | 230 | 220 |

- **Reference models:**
  - GHI_30min / GHI_1h (`pred_Ref.m`, clear-sky-index based): AR-on-CSI, Persistence (P), Smart Persistence (SP), Clear-Sky (CS), CLIPER, Exponential Smoothing (ES), ARTU (`K_alpha.m` + `R0*.mat` lookup tables, second-order autocorrelation-based), and COMB (mean of SP/CLIPER/ES/ARTU).
  - PV, WT, PT, WS, T2M (`pred_Ref_PV_WT_PT_WS_T2M.m`): Persistence (P) and hourly Climatology (CLIM) — matches the "Persistence" / "Climatology" rows of Table IV in the paper.

### 8. Evaluation metrics

**Stationarity diagnostics** (filtering quality, computed on the residual):

- Aggregated PACF: $\mathrm{PACF}(\hat R)=\sum_{k=1}^{K}|\mathrm{PACF}_{\hat R}(k)|$, $K=48$ samples (2 diurnal cycles, hourly) — lower is better.
- Spectral entropy: $H=-\frac{1}{\log N_f}\sum_i P(f_i)\log P(f_i) \in[0,1]$ from the Welch periodogram (`pwelch`) — higher is better (flatter spectrum, weaker periodic forcing).
- Largest Lyapunov Exponent (LLE), Rosenstein method (`estimate_LLE.m`) — lower after preprocessing indicates removed deterministic forcing.

**Forecasting metrics** (predictive value, computed on the reconstructed forecast, `Erreur.m`):

$$\mathrm{nRMSE}=\frac{\sqrt{\frac1N\sum_t \varepsilon_t^2}}{\bar Y},\qquad
\mathrm{nMAE}=\frac{\frac1N\sum_t|\varepsilon_t|}{\bar Y},\qquad
\mathrm{nMBE}=\frac{\frac1N\sum_t \varepsilon_t}{\bar Y}$$

with $\varepsilon_t=\hat Y_t-Y_t$, plus the coefficient of determination $R^2$ and the persistence-normalized **NICE** family:

$$\mathrm{NICE}_k=\frac{\ell_k(\varepsilon)}{\ell_k(\varepsilon_{\text{pers}})},\quad k\in\{1,2,3\},\qquad \mathrm{NICE}_\Sigma=\tfrac13(\mathrm{NICE}_1+\mathrm{NICE}_2+\mathrm{NICE}_3)$$

$\mathrm{NICE}_k<1$ means the model beats simple persistence on the order-$k$ error norm.

## Results summary

Average rank across all 9 variables and 3 stationarity metrics (PACF, spectral entropy, LLE) — lower is better (Table II):

| Method | Average rank |
|---|---|
| MEDIAN | 2.33 |
| LOESS | 2.81 |
| PHASE | 3.07 |
| PROJECTION | 3.89 |
| FOURIER | 4.33 |
| RAW | 4.56 |

Average forecasting rank by nRMSE (best of the EL/AR forecaster per method), over 6 horizons (Table III):

| Method | Solar | Wind | All |
|---|---|---|---|
| PHASE | 2.75 | 2.12 | 2.55 |
| FOURIER | 3.03 | 1.88 | 2.66 |
| RAW | 2.31 | 3.47 | 2.68 |
| PROJECTION | 3.64 | 3.82 | 3.70 |
| MEDIAN | 3.89 | 3.76 | 3.85 |
| LOESS | 5.39 | 5.94 | 5.57 |

The stationarity ranking does not transfer to forecasting: MEDIAN produces the cleanest residuals but is only middling as a forecasting preprocessor; PHASE is the best overall forecasting preprocessor and the only one whose mean gain over RAW stays non-negative across all horizons on both solar and wind variables.
